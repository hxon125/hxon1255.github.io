Punk?

Punk is not just a genre of music, but a subculture that resists the existing order and authority and tries to think for itself. It rebelled against rock music that appeared in the British and American underground in the mid-to-late 1970s and was complicated by short and fast song structures, three to four chords, rough guitar and straight drums.
In particular, 1977 was the year when punk became popular in earnest with the appearance of Sex Pistols and
The Clash, and "Punk "77" is used as a code to symbolize the originality and resistance of early punk. Rather than directly connecting with this traditional punk, Avril Lavigne is a pop punk icon who combined punk's rebellious attitude with pop melodies to extend punk into a genre accessible to the wider generation.
