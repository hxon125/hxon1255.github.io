2002
Debut album “Let Go” released, introducing her skate‑punk attitude to the mainstream.
With singles like “Complicated” and “Sk8er Boi,” she becomes a teen icon and a core voice of early‑2000s pop‑punk.
2004
Second album “Under My Skin” brings a darker, heavier pop‑punk and rock sound.
Lyrics grow more introspective, showing her evolution from rebellious teenager to serious songwriter.
2007
“The Best Damn Thing” era turns her into a full‑on global pop star with louder hooks and cheer‑punk energy.
Catchy anthems and bright visuals cement the “Pop Punk Princess” image in mainstream culture.
2011
The self‑titled era mixes pop‑rock, ballads, and nostalgic punk‑influenced tracks in a more polished sound.
She balances playful, youthful energy with more mature themes about relationships and identity.
2019
“Head Above Water” marks a comeback after years of health struggles, with emotional rock ballads at the core.
Vulnerable lyrics and soaring choruses show a different side of her, without losing her signature intensity.
2022
A new album rides the pop‑punk revival, reconnecting with the raw, fast guitar sound of her early days.
Collaborations with younger rock and pop‑punk artists prove her influence across a new generation.
